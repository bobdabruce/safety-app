OHS Glossary — Acronyms & Definitions

OHS Tools

Thermal Work Limit (TWL)

Calculates the maximum safe metabolic rate (W/m²) for acclimatised workers in a specific thermal environment. Based on Brake & Bates (2002).

W/m² (Watts per square metre) — Thermal Work Limit

TWL Thresholds (AMSB / WA Mines): >220 Low · 160–220 Moderate · 115–160 High · <115 Critical
Assumes acclimatised worker, light work attire (~0.175 clo, im=0.45), globe temp used directly as MRT. Matches Aqualyte/Brake & Bates standard output.

Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT)

Calculates the WBGT heat stress index. Use outdoor formula when working in direct sun or near radiant heat sources. Use indoor formula for enclosed workplaces with no significant solar load.

Formula: WBGT = 0.7TW + 0.2TG + 0.1TD

°C WBGT

ACGIH TLV thresholds — Light: 28/31°C · Moderate: 25/28°C · Heavy: 22.5/25°C · Very Heavy: 20/22.5°C (unacclimatised/acclimatised)

Humidex Heat Stress

Calculates the Humidex index and heat stress risk level for indoor workers. Based on OHCOW / CCOHS guidelines (Masterton & Richardson, 1979).

Humidex

Risk levels: <30 No concern · 30–34 Little discomfort · 35–39 Some discomfort · 40–45 Great discomfort · ≥46 Dangerous

NIOSH/OSHA Heat Index

Calculates the Heat Index (feels-like temperature) and OSHA-NIOSH risk level for outdoor and indoor workers. Based on the Rothfusz regression and OSHA-NIOSH Heat Safety Tool thresholds.

°C Heat Index (feels like)

OSHA-NIOSH risk levels: <33°C Lower · 33–39°C Moderate · 39–46°C High · >46°C Very High/Extreme  |  Source: CDC NIOSH Heat Safety Tool
